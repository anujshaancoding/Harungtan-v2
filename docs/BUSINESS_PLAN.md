## 1. Executive Summary

Harungtan is a Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) premium t-shirt brand for men and women, proudly made in India. The brand targets the growing segment of young, style-conscious Indians aged 18-35 who seek quality apparel at fair prices -- without the markup of legacy retail brands.

India's D2C fashion market is projected to reach $60 billion by 2027, driven by increasing internet penetration, growing preference for online shopping, and a young demographic that values brand story over brand age. Brands like Bewakoof (started with Rs.30,000), The Souled Store, and Bonkers Corner have proven that an online-first clothing brand can scale rapidly with the right product-market fit and digital marketing strategy.

Harungtan will differentiate through:
- **Premium quality fabrics** (180-220 GSM bio-washed cotton) at accessible prices
- **Design-forward aesthetics** -- oversized fits, acid wash, minimal prints, and gender-neutral options
- **Strong digital presence** with community-driven brand building
- **Sustainable practices** -- eco-friendly packaging, responsible sourcing
- **Technology-first approach** -- custom-built e-commerce platform (Next.js + Stripe) for complete control over customer experience

**Year 1 Revenue Target:** Rs.30-50 lakh
**Break-even Timeline:** Month 8-10
**Initial Investment Required:** Rs.3-5 lakh (minimum viable launch)

## 8. Supply Chain Strategy

### Manufacturing Hub: Tirupur, Tamil Nadu

Tirupur is India's knitwear capital, producing over 50% of India's cotton knitwear exports. It offers:
- 6,000+ garment units ranging from small workshops to large factories
- End-to-end capability: spinning, knitting, dyeing, printing, stitching
- Competitive pricing due to cluster economics
- Established quality standards (many units are export-certified)

### Supplier Selection Process

1. **Research Phase (2-3 weeks)**
   - Visit IndiaMart, TradeIndia for initial supplier discovery
   - Contact 15-20 manufacturers, request samples and price lists
   - Join Tirupur garment manufacturer WhatsApp groups

2. **Sample Phase (2-3 weeks)**
   - Order samples from 5-6 shortlisted manufacturers (Rs.500-1,000 per sample)
   - Evaluate: fabric quality, stitching, print quality, wash consistency
   - Check minimum order quantities (MOQ) -- target manufacturers with MOQ of 50-100 pieces per design

3. **Negotiation Phase (1 week)**
   - Negotiate pricing based on volume commitments
   - Establish payment terms (50% advance, 50% on delivery for first order)
   - Get timeline commitments (10-15 days for standard orders)

4. **First Order (2-3 weeks production)**
   - Start with 100-150 pieces across 5-6 designs
   - 3-4 sizes per design (M, L, XL, XXL for men; S, M, L, XL for women)
   - 2-3 colors per design

### Alternative Manufacturing Hubs

| Hub | Specialty | When to Use |
|-----|-----------|-------------|
| Tirupur, Tamil Nadu | Cotton t-shirts, knits | Primary hub for t-shirts |
| Ludhiana, Punjab | Hoodies, sweatshirts, winter wear | When expanding to winter categories |
| Noida/Delhi NCR | Fast fashion, quick turnaround | For rush orders or trend-response pieces |
| Kolkata | Budget manufacturing | For value-segment products |

### Inventory Management Strategy

**Phase 1 (Month 1-3): Just-In-Time Lite**
- Keep 100-200 pieces in stock
- Reorder when stock drops to 30% of initial quantity
- Accept 7-10 day lead time for restocking
- Focus on core/evergreen designs

**Phase 2 (Month 4-8): Data-Driven Inventory**
- Maintain 2-3 weeks of inventory based on sales velocity
- Use sales data to predict demand per SKU
- Eliminate slow-moving SKUs, double down on winners
- Introduce seasonal/limited-edition drops

**Phase 3 (Month 9-12): Scaled Operations**
- Maintain 4-6 weeks of inventory for top sellers
- Pre-order system for new launches
- Safety stock for festive seasons (Diwali, Christmas, New Year)

### Shipping and Logistics

| Service | Purpose | Cost |
|---------|---------|------|
| Shiprocket | Multi-courier aggregator | Rs.35-60/shipment (prepaid), Rs.50-80 (COD) |
| Delhivery (direct) | Reliable for metro cities | Rs.40-65/shipment |

## Appendix

### Key Assumptions
1. Average return rate: 15% (industry standard for online fashion in India)
2. COD orders: 40-50% (decreasing over time with trust-building)
3. Repeat purchase rate improves from 10% to 30% over 12 months
4. CAC decreases as brand awareness grows
5. No external funding assumed -- bootstrapped operations
6. Founder(s) handle operations without salary for first 6 months

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| High return rates | Detailed size guides, video try-ons, quality photos |
| Cash flow crunch from COD | Incentivize prepaid with 5-10% discount |
| Quality inconsistency | Rigorous QC before shipping, manufacturer audits |
| Competition from funded brands | Niche positioning, community building, superior product |
| Rising ad costs | Invest in organic growth (SEO, content, referrals) early |
